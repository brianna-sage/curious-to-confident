Checklist Self-Reflection References

Before guiding your child through sexual development, it’s important to reflect on your own beliefs, experiences, and attitudes. These questions are designed to help you pause, explore honestly, and grow into a more confident, compassionate guide. Your openness and healing can empower your child more than any fact or answer ever could.

  • What biases or judgments do you hold about sexuality?
    How might these affect your tone, language, or comfort level when speaking with your teen?
  • Have you felt safe in your own sexual exploration?
    How might your past experiences shape how you support or restrict your teen’s journey?
  • Are you modeling a sex-positive, shame-free perspective for your teen?
    What messages (verbal or nonverbal) are you currently sending about bodies, boundaries, or relationships?
  • How have you contributed to the sexualization of others through thoughts or actions?
    What changes can you make to promote a more respectful, person-centered view of sexuality in your home?
