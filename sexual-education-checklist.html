Before your child steps into adulthood, these are the conversations and understandings they should carry with confidence. You don't need to cover it all at once—these truths are built slowly, with time, trust, and presence.

  • ✔ I respect my body and understand that I am worthy of dignity and respect from myself and others.
  • ✔ I can look at my body with acceptance, embracing both its strengths and imperfections.
  • ✔ I can identify male and female reproductive anatomy and understand the process of fertilization.
  • ✔ I am familiar with multiple birth control methods and have a plan for responsible use if needed.
  • ✔ I understand consent as something that must be actively given and received—both verbally and nonverbally.
  • ✔ I feel comfortable in my masculinity or femininity and understand that gender expression is personal and unique.
  • ✔ I recognize the risks of pornography and understand how it can shape unrealistic expectations about relationships and intimacy.
  • ✔ I know that my past sexual choices do not define me. I can learn, grow, and be forgiven for any mistakes I’ve made.
  • ✔ I acknowledge that sexual pleasure is a natural and good part of human experience, designed for deep emotional and spiritual connection.
  • ✔ I understand that healthy relationships require open communication, respect, and mutual trust.
